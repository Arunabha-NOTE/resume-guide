Projects

This section will show what your projects section should look like. Projects demonstrate your practical skills and ability to apply your knowledge.

Each project should include the project name, technologies used, a brief description, and key achievements or features.

Example Format:

E-Commerce Platform | Github Link
June 2025
– Built a full-stack e-commerce application with user authentication, product catalog, shopping cart, and payment integration.
– Implemented microservices architecture to enhance scalability and maintainability.
– Tech Stack: Next.js, Redis, TailwindCSS, Spring Boot, PostgreSQL, Docker, Kubernetes, RabbitMQ, Jenkins, Terraform, AWS(ECR, EKS, Aurora, etc).
Introduction to scratch (Conference Paper) | DOI
January 2025
– Wrote a research paper on introduction to scratch and presented it in a conference.
– Tech Stack: Scratch

Do's and Don'ts

Do's

  • Include links to live demos or GitHub repositories when possible.
  • Try to add 3-4 projects and tailor them as per job profile and added skills in your resume.
  • Avoid adding internship projects unless you are desperate or its good enough to mention outside of your experiences section.

Don'ts

  • Don't include basic tutorial projects or coursework projects unless they're significantly enhanced.
  • Don't use vague descriptions; be specific about your contributions and technologies used.
  • Don't add buzz words and fake metrics you saw on certain ATS website and AI suggestions. Add only if you have data to back it up.
