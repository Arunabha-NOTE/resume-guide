Overview

This is a website to be used as a reference for resume writing. This is in no way the perfect resume guide or the final guide — you always have to come up with a solution tailored to you.

I have split each section into three parts:

  • An introduction explaining the section and its purpose.
  • Do's: Best practices and things to include when writing your resume.
  • Dont's: Things to avoid when writing your resume.

Feel free to open issues on GitHub if you want to contribute.

Getting started

For starters, go through this website where I'll discuss do's, don'ts, and what to include or omit for each section.

You can follow along by using any of the following resources to create your resume:

  • Try the popular Sheets Resume templates: sheetsresume.com
  • Use Overleaf to write with LaTeX: overleaf.com
  • For a shortcut, check: monoresume.com

General guidelines

  • For a fresher, keep your CV to one single page.
  • Don't AI generate it man.
  • Order of sections is flexible — tailor it to the job, projects, and skills you want to highlight.
  • Certifications/achievements/extracurriculars are optional — use them as a boost for relevant profiles.
  • Always proofread your resume multiple times and, if possible, have someone else review it to catch any errors or inconsistencies.
  • Please do not fall for things like ATS score and add BS keywords and numbers to your resume.
  • Don't add fake projects or skills you are unsure of everything you write is free game and can and will be used against you in your interviews.
  • Later sections of this site will go into more detail for each resume area.
